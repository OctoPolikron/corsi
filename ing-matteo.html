📖 Grammatica

⏱ Present Continuous

Uso: Azioni che stanno accadendo ORA o azioni future programmate

📝 Struttura

FormaStrutturaEsempio
AffermativaSOGGETTO + am/is/are + VERBO-ingI am studying now.
NegativaSOGGETTO + am/is/are + not + VERBO-ingShe is not coming tomorrow.
InterrogativaAm/Is/Are + SOGGETTO + VERBO-ing?Are you watching TV?

🎯 Esempi

  • I am studying now. - Sto studiando adesso.
  • She is meeting Tom tonight. - Lei incontra Tom stasera.
  • They are not coming tomorrow. - Non vengono domani.
  • Are you watching TV? - Stai guardando la TV?
⚠️ Attenzione: Alcuni verbi NON si usano al continuous: like, love, hate, know, understand, want, need, believe.

🔄 Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Present SimplePresent Continuous
Uso: Routine, abitudini, verità generaliUso: Azioni che accadono ora
I play football every Saturday.I am playing football now.
Parole chiave: always, usually, every day, never, sometimesParole chiave: now, at the moment, look!, listen!, today

🎯 Confronto

  • Simple: She works in a bank. (abitudine)
  • Continuous: She is working now. (ora)
  • Simple: They always eat pasta. (abitudine)
  • Continuous: They are eating pasta now. (ora)

⏪ Past Simple

Uso: Azioni passate completate

📝 Verbi Regolari

FormaEsempio
AffermativaShe walked to school.
NegativaShe didn't walk to school.
InterrogativaDid she walk to school?

⚡ Verbo "to be" al passato

SoggettoPassatoEsempio
I / He / She / ItwasThere was a big park.
You / We / TheywereThey were happy.

🔥 Verbi Irregolari comuni

  • go → went - We went to Rome.
  • see → saw - Did you see the film?
  • eat → ate - I ate pizza yesterday.
  • have → had - She had a dog.
  • do → did - I did my homework.

⏪ Past Continuous

Uso: Azioni in corso nel passato, spesso interrotte da un'altra azione

📝 Struttura

FormaStrutturaEsempio
AffermativaSOGGETTO + was/were + VERBO-ingI was studying when you called.
NegativaSOGGETTO + was/were + not + VERBO-ingShe wasn't sleeping.
InterrogativaWas/Were + SOGGETTO + VERBO-ing?Were you watching TV?

🎯 Quando usarlo

  • Azione in corso: At 8 o'clock yesterday, I was eating dinner.
  • Azione interrotta: I was reading when the phone rang.
  • Due azioni parallele: While I was studying, my brother was playing games.

📊 Comparativi

Aggettivi Monosillabici

BaseComparativoEsempio
fastfasterTom is faster than Anna.
talltallerShe is taller than him.
bigbiggerThis room is bigger.

Aggettivi Polisillabici

BaseComparativoEsempio
beautifulmore beautifulThis flower is more beautiful.
intelligentmore intelligentShe is more intelligent.

Comparativo di Uguaglianza

as + aggettivo + as

  • This film is as good as that one.
  • She is as tall as her mother.

Comparativo di Minoranza

less + aggettivo + than

  • She is less intelligent than him.

Irregolari

  • good → better → the best
  • bad → worse → the worst
  • far → farther/further → the farthest/furthest

🏆 Superlativi

TipoStrutturaEsempio
Monosillabicithe + aggettivo + -estHe is the fastest runner.
Polisillabicithe most + aggettivoShe is the most beautiful girl.

Superlativo Assoluto

very / extremely + aggettivo

  • He is very tall.
  • She is extremely intelligent.

📦 Quantifiers

Some / Any

ParolaUsoEsempio
SOMEFrasi affermativeThere is some milk.
ANYFrasi negative e domandeThere aren't any apples.

Much / Many / A lot of

ParolaUsoEsempio
MUCHSostantivi non numerabiliHow much water?
MANYSostantivi numerabiliHow many cups?
A LOT OFEntrambi (affermative)I have a lot of friends.

Countable / Uncountable Nouns

  • Numerabili: apples, books, cars, students
  • Non numerabili: water, milk, sugar, information, money

⚡ Must / Have to

VerboUsoEsempio
MUSTObbligo personale, regolaYou must wear a helmet.
HAVE TONecessità, obbligo esternoI have to study tonight.
MUSTN'TDivietoYou mustn't run here.
DON'T HAVE TONon è necessarioYou don't have to come.

🎯 Differenza principale

  • MUST: Il parlante sente l'obbligo (soggettivo)
  • HAVE TO: L'obbligo viene dall'esterno (oggettivo)
